What is useful

The useful laws, principles, or theorems are those that . . .
relate the variables in the discipline, subject, topic
relate the variables in examples from the same problem class and type
relate the variables in the problem's givens, situation and solution

Laws, principles or theorems are statements expressing relationships between certain variables.

These relationships are then used to solve the problem, by eliminating
the unwanted variables, and solving for the solution variable(s).
